Ghosh Lab

We develop principled statistical methodology for complex, real-world data — from clinical trials and time series to machine learning and causal inference.

What We Do

Research Areas

01
Time Series Analysis
Statistical theory for nonlinear time series, including polyspectral estimation, quadratic prediction, and resampling methods for sample extremes. Applications span finance and macroeconomic data.
02
Longitudinal & Clinical Data
Nonparametric and semiparametric methods for multi-arm clinical trials with multivariate outcomes. Focus areas include Alzheimer's, Parkinson's disease and power/sample size methodology.
03
Machine Learning & Causal Inference
ML methods for social media bot detection, election prediction, air quality modeling, and causal discovery using graph neural networks and penalized FCI algorithms.
Principal Investigator

Lab Director

DG
Dhrubajyoti Ghosh
Principal Investigator · Assistant Professor

Dr. Ghosh received his Ph.D. in Statistics from Washington University in St. Louis, advised by Soumendra Lahiri and Tucker McElroy. He completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Duke University with Prof. Sheng Luo, developing statistical methods for neurodegenerative disease trials. He joined Kennesaw State University in 2025. His work appears in Bernoulli, Sankhya A, Journal of Multivariate Analysis, Statistics in Medicine, IEEE Transactions on Computational Social Systems, and other leading journals.

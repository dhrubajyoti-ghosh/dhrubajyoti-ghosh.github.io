Research & Background

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Data Science and Analytics at Kennesaw State University. My research spans statistical methodology and its application to complex, real-world problems in biomedical science, social media analysis, and financial data.

My methodological work focuses on time series analysis — including polyspectral estimation and quadratic prediction — as well as nonparametric methods for longitudinal clinical trials. I am particularly interested in developing rigorous statistical tools for multivariate and high-dimensional data.

Prior to joining KSU, I completed a postdoctoral fellowship at Duke University with Prof. Sheng Luo, working on clinical trial methodology for neurodegenerative diseases. I received my Ph.D. from Washington University in St. Louis, advised by Soumendra Lahiri and Tucker McElroy.
